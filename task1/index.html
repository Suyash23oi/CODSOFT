Research & Publications

Publication in Progress ICAISS-2026

"Evaluating Air, Land and Sea-based Nature-Inspired Algorithms for the Travelling Salesman Problem"

Conference: Fourth International Conference on Augmented Intelligence and Sustainable Systems (ICAISS-2026) My Role: Research, Analysis, Documentation, and Presentation

Research Focus

This study evaluates the performance, speed, and accuracy of Nature-Inspired metaheuristic optimization algorithms categorized by environmental domains (Air, Land, and Sea) in solving the NP-hard Travelling Salesman Problem (TSP). Utilizing execution time and execution cost metrics, this study benchmarks their limits under diverse structural scales.

Key Findings & Results

Delivered comprehensive statistical performance comparative data showing that ocean-based heuristics (Whale Optimization) and land-based (Grey Wolf Optimizer) exhibit superior convergence speeds and lower search costs for higher-dimensionality datasets, outperforming air-based models (Dragonfly & Firefly) on complex node sets.
